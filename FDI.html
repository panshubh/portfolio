Analysis of Foreign Direct Investment

Studying the Reserve Bank of India data on FDI from 2000-20, this analysis shows the overall trend of the foreign investments, forecast for the next three years. It highlights the top investor countries and the Indian states with highest investments.

Dataset

The data available on the RBI official website is in one single consolidated excel file.

This consolidated excel file was sliced into multiple other sheets with relevant and important data needed for the analysis. For e.g., Sheets with name of the states and the amount invested, data of investor countries, etc.

Overall trend & Forecast

A steady and linear growth in the FDI can be seen in the below graph, as the FDI has risen from Rs. 4000 crores in year 2000 to almost Rs. 74,000 crores in 2019.

Although there was a small plunge in the value in year 2008 and it took three years to recover and coming back on track, we can conclude that the investor trust and appeal is grown for India over time looking at the opportunities.

Sector-wise division

The treemap chart gives us a clear picture of the sectors which have attracted the highest investments. The early 2000s saw highest number of investors in the computer software & hardware sector but soon the position was overtaken by the Service sector, which is leading the way for last three years.

Analyzing last Fiscal year (2020-21)

The month of August has seen astronomical rise in the investments in spite of the COVID-19 crisis around the globe. Many foreign companies have shown interest in the Indian market and are investing heavily to migrate their production and business processes from China considering the changing trade policies.

Top Investors for India

Comparing the data from last three years this bar chart gives us insights on the top 10 countries who have shown a good response and shown interest in the Indian market.

Sate-wise division

Clearly the state of Maharashtra, Gujarat and Karnataka top the list with highest number of investors.
